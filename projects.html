Projects that I have been envolved

An IoT energy consumption system based on Powerline communication.

This work was result of my masters. It can be splitted into circuit design/firmware development and web service application. The circuit shown below uses a microcontroller MSP430AFE253 from Texas Instruments (TI), a switching power supply based on UCC28910 (TI), analog circuits to measure voltage and current, to modulate and demodulate measured data and set it properly in powerline.

Firmware programming was made to acquire the required samples of voltage and current, output PWM carrier signal and digitalized data (UART) to modulation circuit, control the amplifier gain of the demodulation stage and select the adequate carrier frequency by sensing the channel noise.

In order to verify the waveform of the measured signal, I did a test bench using LABVIEW, as shown below.

Finally, the modulation and demodulation circuit was duplicated and attrached on Raspberry Pi. Both module was connected to the powerline, and the measured digitalized data was received by Raspberry Pi and sent to a Flask webservice based on REST using python.

Circuit design for autonomous sensor in industry 4.0

This work was done in public-private approach with a local company and subsidized by FAPESP. The main objective was evaluate the application of thermal Energy Harvesting to supply and maintain an autonomous wireless sensor node. I was responsible to design a power management circuit that interfaces the thermoelectric generator and the wireless sensor node. Three DC-DC converter was evaluated: LTC3108 (Analog Devices), MP3414 (Monolithic Power Systems) and BQ25570 (TI). The circuit shown below is a DC-DC converter using LTC3108 that presented the best performance in our evaluation. A supercapacitor was used to store the harvested energy and to supply the autonomous sensor.

Winner of the 2ª CubeDesign! Cubsat project

I participated of the 2ª CubeDesign Cubsat project organizated by National Institute for Space Research (INPE) by means of participating in an extracurricular team at Unicamp in space-modeling. In this project, I was responsible for Energy harvesting module. The energy harvested by solar panels was used to recharge the Cubesat's battery pack. The current and the voltage of the bateries was sensed and acquired by STM32F103.The Cubesat developed was subjected to a series of tests, including sensor telemetry, control of a reaction wheel and antenna ejection. The team that best demonstrated results was ours. As a result we received first place.
